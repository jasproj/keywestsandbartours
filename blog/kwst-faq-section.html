Key West Sandbar Tours — FAQ

Everything you need to know before you book.

Pricing

How much do sandbar tours cost in Key West? ＋

Key West sandbar tours range from $85–$178 per person for shared group trips to $375–$980 for the full boat on private charters.

Tour Type Typical Price
Shared group sandbar tour $85–$178 / person
Private half-day charter (up to 6) $375–$600 / boat
Tiki boat sandbar trip $375–$525 / boat
Private full-day charter $600–$980 / boat
Sandbar + kayak eco tour $130 / person

Private charters become cost-competitive with shared tours once you have 4+ people splitting the boat price.

How much does a sunset cruise cost in Key West? ＋

Private sunset boat charters run $375–$450 for 2 hours (up to 6 guests). Combo sunset + sandbar trips cost $450–$700 for 4–6 hours.

The 6-hour Sandbar | Snorkel | Sunset combo is $700. The Tiki Boat sunset sandbar trip runs $525. Shared sunset sails start around $50–$89 per person.

Private charter vs. group sandbar tour — which is better value? ＋

Shared tours ($85–$178/person) run on fixed schedules with other guests — typically 6–15 people per boat. Better for solo travelers and couples.

Private charters ($375–$980/boat) depart on your schedule, let you choose your sandbars, and allow BYOB coolers. For groups of 4–6 splitting the cost, private charters often run $62–$130 per person — comparable to or cheaper than a shared tour.

Quick math: a $525 private charter split 6 ways = $87.50 per person, with full flexibility.

Best Time to Go

What is the best time of day for a sandbar tour? ＋

Morning (9–11 AM departures): Calmer water, better underwater visibility, cooler air temperatures, less crowded sandbars. Best option for snorkeling add-ons.

Afternoon (1–3 PM): Works well, though trade winds pick up in summer afternoons. Good for visitors who want a later start.

Sunset (4–6 PM departures): Best for photos and ambiance. Private sunset sandbar charters run 2–4 hours and cost $375–$700. Evening water temperatures are warmest. Peak demand — book in advance.

What is the best time of year for sandbar tours in Key West? ＋

November–April (peak season): Best weather — lower humidity, calmer seas, water temps 72–78°F. Highest demand, especially December–April. Book private charters 1–2 weeks ahead.

May–October (shoulder/summer): Warmer water (84–88°F), fewer crowds outside school breaks. Afternoon thunderstorms are common but usually brief. Tours run in light rain since you're swimming anyway.

Tours run year-round. Operators cancel for safety in rough conditions (high winds, lightning) and typically issue refunds or reschedule.

How long are Key West sandbar tours? ＋

Most sandbar tours run 4 hours — the standard length for reaching backcountry sandbars, spending 90+ minutes swimming, and returning. Options by duration:

  • 2–2.5 hours: Sandbar Express trips — $375–$449 for the boat. One or two sandbar stops.
  • 4–5 hours: Standard sandbar tours — $85–$800 depending on shared vs. private. Most popular length.
  • 6 hours: Combo tours (sandbar + snorkel + sunset) — $550–$700.
  • 8 hours (full day): Comprehensive charters — $600–$980. Multiple sandbars, snorkeling reefs, dolphin watching.

Booking

How far in advance should I book a sandbar tour? ＋

Shared group tours: Book 2–3 days ahead minimum. Popular morning slots fill faster.

Private charters: Book 1–2 weeks ahead during peak season (December–April, summer holidays). Sunset slots and weekends fill the fastest.

As a rule: if you know your travel dates, book as soon as you confirm. Same-day availability exists but isn't reliable, especially for private charters.

What should I bring on a sandbar tour? ＋
  • Reef-safe sunscreen — required in Florida Keys waters (no oxybenzone/octinoxate)
  • Towel and change of clothes
  • Sunglasses and a hat
  • Water shoes or sandals with a back strap
  • Waterproof phone case or dry bag for valuables
  • Extra water and snacks (especially on 4+ hour tours)
  • Snorkel gear (some tours provide it — check your listing)
  • On private charters: your own cooler with food and drinks (BYOB is typically fine)
Are sandbar tours suitable for kids? ＋

Yes. Sandbars are shallow (knee- to waist-deep) with calm, protected water — among the safest water activities in Key West for children. Most tours welcome kids; check individual listings for age or weight minimums.

Family-friendly options: Sandbar & Dolphin Adventure ($130/person, 5 hrs) and Sandbar & Kayak Eco Adventure ($130/person, 5 hrs). Private charters let you set the pace for the whole group.

Morning vs. sunset boat tour in Key West — which should I book? ＋

Choose morning if: you want calm water, better snorkeling visibility, cooler temperatures, and more time for exploring multiple sandbars. Morning tours have 70 sandbar options in the data vs. 12 evening options.

Choose sunset if: you want the best photos, warmest water temperature, and a more social atmosphere. The 6-hour Sandbar | Snorkel | Sunset combo ($700) covers both. Standalone sunset sandbar charters run $375–$525 for the boat.

What is included in a Key West sandbar tour? ＋

Most tours include: licensed captain, life jackets, and one or more sandbar stops. What varies by operator:

  • Snorkel gear — included on the Snorkel & Sandbar Quickie ($583) and Sandbar & Kayak Eco Adventure ($130)
  • Kayaks or paddleboards — available on eco-adventure tours
  • Dolphin watching — included on the Sandbar & Dolphin Adventure ($130, 5 hrs)
  • Cooler with ice — standard on most private charters (you bring the drinks)
  • Bottled water — typically included on shared tours

Always check the specific tour listing for exact inclusions before booking.
