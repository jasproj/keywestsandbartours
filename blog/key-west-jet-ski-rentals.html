Nothing beats the thrill of blasting across Key West's turquoise waters on a jet ski. Whether you want to explore hidden mangrove channels, race to remote sandbars, or just feel the salt spray on your face, jet skiing is one of the most exhilarating ways to experience the Florida Keys.

Guided Tours vs. Rentals

Guided Jet Ski Tours ‚Äî A guide leads your group through scenic routes, pointing out wildlife and stopping at sandbars. Best for first-timers and those who want to see the highlights without navigation stress.

Freestyle Rentals ‚Äî Rent a jet ski and go wherever you want within designated areas. More freedom, but you need to know where you're going. Usually restricted to certain zones.

Our recommendation: Guided tours for first-time visitors. You'll see more, go further, and discover spots you'd never find on your own.

  • Book morning trips: Water is calmest early. Afternoon winds can make it choppy.
  • Wear sunscreen before arrival: You'll be in full sun with water reflection. SPF 50+ minimum.
  • Secure everything: Sunglasses with croakies, waterproof phone case, nothing loose in pockets.
  • Bring a towel: You will get wet. Some operators provide towels, some don't.
  • No experience needed: Jet skis are easy to operate. Brief training provided.

Requirements & Restrictions

  • Minimum age to drive: Usually 16-18 with valid ID
  • Passengers: Kids of any age can ride as passengers
  • License: No boating license required in Florida
  • Swimming ability: Must be able to swim (life jackets provided)
  • Weight limits: Typically 400-500 lbs per ski

What to Wear

  • Swimsuit (you will get wet)
  • Water shoes or sandals that strap on
  • Sunglasses with strap
  • Rash guard or light shirt (sun protection)
  • Leave jewelry, watches, and valuables behind

Best Time for Jet Skiing

Time of day: Morning (8-11am) for calmest water. Avoid midday heat and afternoon storms.

Season: Year-round in Key West. Spring (March-May) offers ideal conditions. Summer can be hot but water is warmest. Winter occasionally has choppy days.
