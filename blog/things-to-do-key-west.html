25 Best Things to Do in Key West 2025

Updated January 2025 ‚Ä¢ 12 min read

Key West packs more adventure per square mile than anywhere else in Florida. America's southernmost city blends Caribbean charm, world-class water sports, legendary nightlife, and that famous laid-back attitude into one unforgettable destination.

üåä Water Adventures

1. Snorkel the Only Living Coral Reef in the Continental US
Crystal-clear water, colorful fish, sea turtles‚Äîthe Florida Reef is unmissable and just minutes offshore.
2. Visit a Secret Sandbar
Stand in waist-deep turquoise water miles from shore. Local captains know hidden sandbars where you'll feel like you discovered your own private island.
3. Jet Ski the Backcountry
Blast through mangrove channels and open water. Most trips include sandbar stops and dolphin sightings.
4. Kayak Through Mangrove Tunnels
Paddle through natural mangrove "tunnels" in the Key West Wildlife Refuge. Spot herons, rays, and baby sharks.
5. Go Deep Sea Fishing
Chase mahi-mahi, sailfish, and tuna. Key West is one of the top sportfishing destinations in the world.
6. Parasail Over the Island
Soar 300-500 feet above Key West for bird's-eye views of the island, reefs, and endless blue water.
7. Rent a Boat for the Day
Captain your own vessel to sandbars, snorkel spots, and secret beaches. No license required for most rentals.

üåÖ Sunset Experiences

8. Sunset Sail on a Catamaran
Champagne, live music, and Key West's legendary sunset from the water. The most popular evening activity.
9. Watch Sunset at Mallory Square
Free nightly celebration with street performers, artists, and food vendors. The crowd cheers when the sun touches the horizon.
10. Private Romantic Sunset Cruise
Just you and your person on a private charter. Perfect for proposals and anniversaries.

11. Dolphin Watching Tour
Wild bottlenose dolphins live here year-round. Morning tours have 90%+ sighting rates.
12. Day Trip to Dry Tortugas
70 miles west, this remote national park has pristine beaches, incredible snorkeling, and a massive Civil War fort.
13. Key West Butterfly Conservatory
Walk through a tropical garden with 50+ butterfly species and exotic birds.

88 steps to panoramic views of the island.

The iconic marker 90 miles from Cuba. Arrive early morning to avoid lines.
